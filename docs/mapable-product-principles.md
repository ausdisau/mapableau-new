# MapAble product principles

These principles guide product, engineering, and content decisions across MapAble verticals.

## 1. Disability-led and rights-based

MapAble must respect autonomy, choice, control, privacy, dignity, and diverse communication preferences.

## 2. Accessibility by default

Every page, component, and workflow should aim for WCAG 2.2 AA or better. Plain language is a feature, not an afterthought.

## 3. Ecosystem-native

New features should strengthen MapAble Core, the accessibility map, and service coordination across care, transport, jobs, home, community, and civic access.

## 4. Consent first

Sensitive information must be private by default, shareable only with clear consent, revocable where possible, and understandable in plain language.

## 5. Human review for high-risk workflows

AI and automation may assist with matching, triage, summaries, and suggestions, but must not silently decide high-impact issues such as safety, eligibility, complaints, payments, or sensitive disclosures.

## 6. Evidence over vague claims

Where MapAble shows access information, prefer exact details, measurements, dates, photos, reviewer context, and assessment status over vague labels like "accessible."

## 7. Operational realism

Avoid launching verticals that require heavy physical operations before validating demand, safety, compliance, and partner capacity.

## 8. Trust is the product

A failure in one MapAble vertical can damage the entire ecosystem. Design for reliability, auditability, respectful communication, and graceful failure.

## 9. NDIS-aware, not NDIS-dependent

MapAble should support NDIS-aligned workflows where appropriate but also build B2B, B2G, aged care, employer, venue, tourism, and community revenue pathways.

## 10. No replacement for emergency, clinical, or legal support

MapAble can organise information and suggest pathways, but must not present itself as emergency services, legal advice, clinical advice, or a funding decision-maker.
